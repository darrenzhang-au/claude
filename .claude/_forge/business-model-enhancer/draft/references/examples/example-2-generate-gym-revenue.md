# Golden Example 2 — Generate branch (goal only, no specific idea)

## Input
> "I run a local gym, membership is $40/month. I want a new revenue stream but I don't have a specific idea.
> What should I add?"

(Generate branch: goal given, no candidate. Customer = local members, revenue = $40/mo membership. Cost
driver and capacity not stated.)

## A great output asserts
- **Intake:** asks the missing anchors — main cost driver (rent/staff, largely fixed), capacity/utilization,
  and the goal behind "new revenue" (margin vs. growth).
- **Anchor:** Model snapshot naming the gym's fixed-cost + capacity reality (a full class at peak has no
  marginal capacity; off-peak is idle).
- **Divergence — one candidate per lens:**
  - Pricing/packaging → premium/peak tier or day-pass.
  - Segment → corporate memberships / physio referrals.
  - Channel → online/on-demand classes for non-local members.
  - Product depth → personal training, nutrition coaching.
  - Partnership → physio / supplement / local employer tie-ins.
  - Business-model shift → e.g. class-pack transactions vs. flat membership.
  - At least one lens marked **N/A — reason** if it genuinely doesn't fit.
- **Independent pre-mortems** tied to gym economics (capacity limits, local TAM ceiling, staff cost, member
  cannibalization of the flat fee).
- **Scored table**, **one recommendation** + runner-up tradeoff, **cheapest first-test** (e.g. sell 10
  corporate passes to one nearby employer before building anything).

## A bad output (fails)
- Ideas untethered from a gym's fixed-cost/capacity reality ("launch an app!" with no economics).
- Three variants of "add more classes".
- No N/A handling; every lens forced into a weak candidate.
- No decision, or invented revenue numbers.

## Why
Proves the generate path: lens-driven distinct candidates, N/A handling, and anchoring to a fixed-cost
capacity-bound model — then still converging on one testable bet.
