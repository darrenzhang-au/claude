# Strategic Lenses — the divergence engine

Generate **one candidate per lens** (Workflow step 3). The point of a fixed lens set is to force genuinely
*different* bets instead of variations on one idea. If a lens does not fit the model, mark it
**N/A — <reason>** rather than forcing a weak candidate.

| # | Lens | The question it asks | Example move | Typically N/A when… |
|---|------|----------------------|--------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **New pricing / packaging** | Can the same value be captured differently? | Add a premium tier; usage-based add-on; annual prepay; bundle two products | Price is externally fixed / regulated |
| 2 | **New segment** | Who else has this problem and would pay? | Sell the freelancer tool to small agencies; add an enterprise SKU | The offering is inherently single-segment |
| 3 | **New channel / distribution** | Where else could it reach buyers? | Marketplace listing, embedded/API, reseller, in-person, self-serve web | Channel is the fixed core of the model already |
| 4 | **Product / feature depth** | What adjacent job could it also do? | Add a reporting layer, an AI assist, an integration, a workflow step | The product is deliberately single-purpose and constrained |
| 5 | **Partnership / ecosystem** | Whose customers or assets could you borrow? | Bundle with a complementary provider; co-sell; referral/affiliate | No credible complementary partners exist |
| 6 | **Business-model shift** | Could the *mechanism* of value/capture change? | Subscription → transaction fee; product → platform; one-off → recurring; service → productized | The current mechanism is the moat and shifting destroys it |

## How to use each lens
1. Read the model snapshot (Workflow 2).
2. For each lens, ask its question against *this* model and produce the single strongest candidate — named,
   concrete, and tied to the snapshot's customers/economics.
3. If the lens's "typically N/A when" applies, write `N/A — <reason>` and move on. Aim for ≥4 live candidates;
   fewer than 3 live lenses usually means the intake was too thin — go back and anchor harder.

## Evaluate-branch note
When the user brought a *specific* idea, don't discard the lenses — refract the idea through them. The same
"add AI" idea becomes distinct bets: a **pricing** play (AI as a premium tier), a **segment** play (AI that
opens a new buyer), a **partnership** play (integrate a model vendor), a **business-model shift** (AI usage
metered per call). That is how you turn one idea into genuinely different variants to stress-test.
