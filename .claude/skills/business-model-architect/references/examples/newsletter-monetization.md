# Golden Example 2 — Audience exists, but NO revenue model (in-scope zero-to-one)

## Input
> "I've got a newsletter with 5,000 subscribers making $0. What's the best way to make money from it?"

## A great output asserts
- **Correctly classifies this as Architect territory, not Enhancer** — there's an audience but **no revenue model yet**, so it does NOT redirect. (Contrast with Example 3.)
- Confirms ambition if unstated (a $2k/mo side income vs. a venture media business changes the answer) before committing.
- **Converges on ONE monetization model** (e.g., paid premium tier vs. sponsorships/ads vs. a productized offering) matched to the subscriber count and ambition — with a clear reason it beats the others at *this* scale (5k subs is thin for pure sponsorship CPMs; a paid tier or product may monetize the same audience harder).
- **Names the runner-up + tradeoff**, the **riskiest assumption** (e.g., "≥3-5% will convert to paid"), and a **cheap validation** (soft-launch a paid tier to the list, or run one sponsor slot).
- Unverified conversion/CPM numbers are labeled, not asserted.

## A bad output has
- Redirecting to `business-model-enhancer` (wrong — no model exists to enhance).
- Listing every monetization option without picking one.
- Quoting conversion rates or CPMs as if they were known facts for this list.

**Why:** guards the subtle boundary — an existing *asset* (audience) is not an existing *model*. This stays in scope and must still converge.
